      You reach back into your memory, trying to gather up everything you need to handle Kyouko properly and...
      *SCREEECH*
      'NO! NO! KYOUKO! LOOK AT ME! PLEASE!'
      *CLANG*
      She continues dancing, ignorant of your presence...ignorant of you. You're just a girl with braids, not her dearest friend and confidante. Not her...
      *THUD*
      'PLEASE!!! I NEED YOU! KYOUKO!'

      Your eyes are burning. You can't quite make out the girls in front of you anymore.

      'She...she doesn't...I can't...please...'
      There's a hand on your shoulder.
      You breathe deeply, gathering your will to slam the emotions back into line. You can cry later. For now, you need to stay focused.

      Kyouko is dancing hard, and Sayaka is pushing back with everything she has, but...she's only human. She can't beat Kyouko in this state.

      You feel your pulse speeding up as you watch Kyouko showing off. She's always been the passionate one. The one who would rather push herself too far than give up while it's safe. She's done that for you before.

      That passion is alight on her face now, too. She's enjoying this, Sayaka's ferocious struggle pushing the redhead to draw upon her magic more heavily.

      You know the blueberry can't win. Kyouko's too good, and too competitive to lose.

      You open your mouth to make a smartass comment and get a smirk out of her, but grab a hold of yourself just in time.

      Sayaka. Sayaka takes priority, this loop.

      You force a smile as you look over at the blue haired girl, "Keep going, Sayaka-chan! Give it your all!"

      She smiles at you midstep, and soon forges on with redoubled effort.

      Kyouko also glances back and smirks confidently. Your heart cracks...and then your actual heart skips a beat.

      Your left arm is numb.

      'What's going on...'

      Your vision is blurring, but you recall your cardiology textbooks.

      This is a heart attack. Thankfully, you're a Magus.

      You detach from your body and pump Healing and Enchantment magic into your heart.

      Everything returns to normal, but Kyouko just did a double take.

      'Shit. She knows.'

      She gives you a long glance, giving Sayaka a moment to pull ahead, before jumping back into the game.

      They continue playing for another round or two, drawing in a large crowd with their exuberance, and the elder Miki finds her way to you, "Hmm...they're really going at it. We can't stay here all day, though. We already bought tickets."

      You nod, waiting for the song to end, before clearing your throat, "Um, Sayaka? I'm getting pretty hungry? Can we go?"

      She pauses, glancing at Kyouko, before looking back at you, "Sure. I think I've made my point. Let's go."

      She steps down and grabs your arm, gently leading you towards the exit...and away from Kyouko.

      You can feel a scream welling up inside you as you start to lose sight of her, but Sayaka's mother apparently knows how to read you already, winking at you before speaking up, "Ah, Sayaka? Perhaps we should invite your new friend? She seems a little lonely."

      Sayaka raises an eyebrow, "Huh? That jerk? She was bullying that kid for no good reason!"

      The older woman nods, "Mmhm, and she might not be so eager to give people trouble if she wasn't alone. Or did you miss the fact that no one was cheering for her?"

      Sayaka pouts, only to perk up and look at you, "Wait! Homura-chan, it's your day! What do you think? We totally should just leave her there!"

      You choke back a snarl, forcing yourself to remember that this isn't the same Kyouko you knew...even if it is, in a way. You glance back at her. She's having fun with her new opponent, but...her loneliness isn't that hard to spot, if you know her well enough.

      Well, that, or you're Mrs. Miki. She can even read you. You wonder if she could give you tips on that.

      "...um, it's up to you, but...she does seem very lonely, and she seems to like you, in a way. No trash talk, no insults. I think she actually enjoyed that. Didn't you?"

      Sayaka glances between you and her mother before sighing loudly, "Dammit. Why do I have to be surrounded by wise people? Can't I just be inconsiderate for once...? Fine."

      She walks back in, glancing at Kyouko, "Hey, crazy, you hungry? I think the other girls would like a chance to play some."

      Kyouko stills at those words, seemingly forgetting the game she's playing altogether, casting a probing look at Sayaka. She seems slightly irritated, but there's definite hope there. The look she throws you a moment later is more guarded, but you let yourself smile at her. She blushes.

      You wonder just how bright that smile was, to get such a reaction.

      'She...she doesn't...I can't...please...'
      There's a hand on your shoulder.
      You breathe deeply, gathering your will to slam the emotions back into line. You can cry later. For now, you need to stay focused.

      Kyouko is dancing hard, and Sayaka is pushing back with everything she has, but...she's only human. She can't beat Kyouko in this state.

      You feel your pulse speeding up as you watch Kyouko showing off. She's always been the passionate one. The one who would rather push herself too far than give up while it's safe. She's done that for you before.

      That passion is alight on her face now, too. She's enjoying this, Sayaka's ferocious struggle pushing the redhead to draw upon her magic more heavily.

      You know the blueberry can't win. Kyouko's too good, and too competitive to lose.

      You open your mouth to make a smartass comment and get a smirk out of her, but grab a hold of yourself just in time.

      Sayaka. Sayaka takes priority, this loop.

      You force a smile as you look over at the blue haired girl, "Keep going, Sayaka-chan! Give it your all!"

      She smiles at you midstep, and soon forges on with redoubled effort.

      Kyouko also glances back and smirks confidently. Your heart cracks...and then your actual heart skips a beat.

      Your left arm is numb.

      'What's going on...'

      Your vision is blurring, but you recall your cardiology textbooks.

      This is a heart attack. Thankfully, you're a Magus.

      You detach from your body and pump Healing and Enchantment magic into your heart.

      Everything returns to normal, but Kyouko just did a double take.

      'Shit. She knows.'

      She gives you a long glance, giving Sayaka a moment to pull ahead, before jumping back into the game.

      They continue playing for another round or two, drawing in a large crowd with their exuberance, and the elder Miki finds her way to you, "Hmm...they're really going at it. We can't stay here all day, though. We already bought tickets."

      You nod, waiting for the song to end, before clearing your throat, "Um, Sayaka? I'm getting pretty hungry? Can we go?"

      She pauses, glancing at Kyouko, before looking back at you, "Sure. I think I've made my point. Let's go."

      She steps down and grabs your arm, gently leading you towards the exit...and away from Kyouko.

      You can feel a scream welling up inside you as you start to lose sight of her, but Sayaka's mother apparently knows how to read you already, winking at you before speaking up, "Ah, Sayaka? Perhaps we should invite your new friend? She seems a little lonely."

      Sayaka raises an eyebrow, "Huh? That jerk? She was bullying that kid for no good reason!"

      The older woman nods, "Mmhm, and she might not be so eager to give people trouble if she wasn't alone. Or did you miss the fact that no one was cheering for her?"

      Sayaka pouts, only to perk up and look at you, "Wait! Homura-chan, it's your day! What do you think? We totally should just leave her there!"

      You choke back a snarl, forcing yourself to remember that this isn't the same Kyouko you knew...even if it is, in a way. You glance back at her. She's having fun with her new opponent, but...her loneliness isn't that hard to spot, if you know her well enough.

      Well, that, or you're Mrs. Miki. She can even read you. You wonder if she could give you tips on that.

      "...um, it's up to you, but...she does seem very lonely, and she seems to like you, in a way. No trash talk, no insults. I think she actually enjoyed that. Didn't you?"

      Sayaka glances between you and her mother before sighing loudly, "Dammit. Why do I have to be surrounded by wise people? Can't I just be inconsiderate for once...? Fine."

      She walks back in, glancing at Kyouko, "Hey, crazy, you hungry? I think the other girls would like a chance to play some."

      Kyouko stills at those words, seemingly forgetting the game she's playing altogether, casting a probing look at Sayaka. She seems slightly irritated, but there's definite hope there. The look she throws you a moment later is more guarded, but you let yourself smile at her. She blushes.

      You wonder just how bright that smile was, to get such a reaction.
